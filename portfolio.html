Project : Landing Page, Blog, and Portfolio Page

Previous Next

The landing page was inspired by me wanting a light and neutral background so the focus for users could be my work. I started with a teal background but found that my work didnt stand out as much. Frustrated, I took to Adobe Colors to find a color scheme that contained more neutral tones. The color scheme that I finally chose ended up being based on sandy colors with green accents

I wanted the website to have a sense of structure so I used containers and cards to organize the website a certain way. For the landing page, I wanted to have seperate sections to display my education, experience and hobbies. So I ended up using formatted containers.

I had a big jumbotron at the start of the page becuase I wanted my name to stand out to website users. The jumbotron also has a tagline that served to attract users to the page.

I decided to add cards to the end of the landing page because I wanted to provide a peek into my work. I wanted to create a section where my blog posts and eventually, projects could be displayed.

I structured my blog simply and with one page so that it would have a continous flow. I wanted to have a big section that displayed the most popular post on the top and then more posts on the left. I added hyperlinks so users could find the post they wanted to read.

I decided to make the website simple and easily accessible for viewers who wanted to take a look at my work. This website was the result.
